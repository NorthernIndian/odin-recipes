Moose Stew Recipe

Description

This is my favorite dish to make during the colder months in Alberta. It warms the soul, and goes down especially good with some scotch and a hot pepper. When your bowl is empty, make sure you clean it up with some sourdough bread.

Ingredients:

  • moose meat
  • 10 baby potatoes
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • salt & pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp of butter
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 heaping tbsp of tomatoe paste
  • 1 cup of beer or red wine
  • 1 tbsp of flour
  • 3 carrots
  • 2 stalks of celery
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms
  • 1-2 tbsp of rosemary or thyme

Steps:

  1. Salt/pepper and flour the meat.
  2. Brown the meat on Medium heat for 8-10 minutes.
  3. Remove meat from pan. Reduce heat to medium-low, add butter and then mushrooms. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add onion and celery. Cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Season the mixture with salt/pepper/parsley and add garlic.Cook for 1-2 minutes.
  6. Season the mixture with salt/pepper/parsley and add garlic.Cook for 1-2 minutes.
  7. Add flour and cook for a minute.
  8. Add tomato paste and cook for another minute.
  9. Add wine or beer and let it cook for 2 minutes.
  10. Add bouillon and browned stew meat. Cook for another minute.
  11. Add vegetables and mix well.
  12. Slowly add beef broth and add bay leaves.
  13. Let it simmer for 1-2 hours on low heat, stirring occasionally.
